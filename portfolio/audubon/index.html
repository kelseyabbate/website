Audubon Birds and Climate Report 2.0

Client
National Audubon Society
Agency
Deep Fried Advertising
Years Active
2019
Role
Lead Design
Work
Report

An 72-page scientific report from Audubon that shows how two-thirds of North American bird species are at risk of extinction from climate change. This report outlines the specific climate-change related threats that birds face and the migration / breeding patterns of 389 species under different climate change scenarios within the coming decades. With immediate action 76% of those species could improve. The report was designed in both English and Spanish.

The design of the report focuses on the various scientific charts and data figures mingled with Audubon's beautiful photos of birds at risk from climate change. A key area of the report features the different threats with colorful spreads for sea/lake level rise, urbanization, cropland expansion, extreme spring heat, fire weather, spring droughts, heavy rainfall, and false springs.

The full report was printed as well as posted online on the official Audubon website.
